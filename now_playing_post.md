# LinkedIn post — Now Playing widget

I've been bothered by my own portfolio for weeks.

The work's there. The writing's there. But every time I opened it, it felt weirdly lifeless. Like a really well-organized closet. Everything in its place, nothing happening.

So this weekend I added a small thing to the bottom-left corner of siladityaa.com that shows what I'm listening to. One line of monospace text. The song. The artist. A tiny speaker icon next to it.

It pulls from my Last.fm scrobbles, mostly. There's also a track I can pin manually when I want to flex something specific, and a backup list it falls through to if everything else breaks. The widget literally cannot show an error. It just always knows what I'm listening to, even if it's a guess.

You can tap the speaker icon and get a 30-second preview from iTunes. The icon goes orange while it plays. Audio cuts off if you change pages, switch tabs, or close the window. Polling stops too when you're not looking. The timestamps are written like a person would talk. JUST NOW. 12M AGO. ON REPEAT. YESTERDAY.

Most people who visit the site won't notice any of this. And honestly, that's fine. The few who do tend to be the ones I want to talk to anyway.

A portfolio is more like a self-portrait than a product. Mine needed a pulse. And, since I think about music way too much, a soundtrack.

(For the record, my Last.fm has been stuck on Runaway by Kanye for three weeks. I don't know what that says about me, but I'm leaving it.)
